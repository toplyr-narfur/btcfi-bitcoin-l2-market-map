# BTCFi / Bitcoin L2 Market Map

A compact public sample for funds, wallet teams, and infra operators tracking Bitcoin security layers, liquid BTC wrappers, and Bitcoin-linked execution environments.

## Why this matters
BTCFi is growing fast, but the landscape is still noisy. Some projects are clearly emerging as capital or security hubs, while others are better understood as execution layers, ecosystem infrastructure, or earlier-stage experiments. The useful question is not just which names are getting attention, but where Bitcoin-linked capital, activity, and strategic positioning are actually concentrating.

## Core takeaways
1. **Babylon and Lombard stand out as current traction anchors** because they sit closest to Bitcoin security participation and productive BTC capital formation. [Babylon](https://babylonlabs.io/) · [Lombard](https://www.lombard.finance/)
2. **Stacks, Rootstock, and BOB are useful execution-layer comparables** for tracking where Bitcoin-linked apps and infrastructure are being positioned. [Stacks](https://www.stacks.co/) · [Rootstock](https://rootstock.io/) · [BOB](https://www.gobob.xyz/)
3. **Liquid still matters strategically** even if it does not fit neatly into DeFi-style TVL comparisons; its role is closer to Bitcoin capital markets and settlement infrastructure. [Liquid Network](https://liquid.net/)
4. **The main challenge is metric quality, not category importance.** A useful BTCFi brief should separate durable traction from noisier headline numbers.
