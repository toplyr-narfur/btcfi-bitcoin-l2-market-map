A compact public sample for funds, wallet teams, and infra operators tracking Bitcoin security layers, liquid BTC wrappers, and Bitcoin-linked execution environments.

## Why this exists
BTCFi coverage is still fragmented. Some projects are traction anchors, some are execution-layer comparables, and some are early or noisy signals that need careful framing. This sample separates those buckets with public positioning sources and public metric references.

## Core takeaways
1. **Babylon and Lombard look like the clearest current traction anchors** in this sample because they sit close to BTC security and productive BTC capital formation. Babylon’s official homepage currently shows `49,658.43 BTC` staked / `$3.34B` and frames itself around self-custodial Bitcoin staking and Bitcoin-secured networks, while Lombard positions itself around bringing Bitcoin capital markets onchain. [Babylon](https://babylonlabs.io/) · [Lombard](https://www.lombard.finance/)
2. **Stacks, Rootstock, and BOB are the most useful current execution-layer comparables** for buyer-facing monitoring. Source framing: Stacks positions itself as the leading Bitcoin L2 for smart contracts and apps, Rootstock as a Bitcoin smart-contract platform, and BOB as a Bitcoin-oriented execution venue. [Stacks](https://www.stacks.co/) · [Rootstock](https://rootstock.io/) · [BOB](https://www.gobob.xyz/)
3. **Babylon’s own official pages already expose a derivative research product angle:** the dedicated ecosystem and finality-provider pages break the network into buyer-relevant categories such as BTC L2s, DeFi, liquid (re)staking, wallets/custodians, rollup infrastructure, Bitcoin Supercharged Networks, and Finality Providers. [Babylon Ecosystem](https://babylonlabs.io/ecosystem) · [Finality Providers](https://babylonlabs.io/finality-providers)
4. **Metric-definition ambiguity is part of the opportunity.** Babylon’s homepage highlights `250+ Finality Providers` while the dedicated Finality Providers page visibly lists `128` across the globe, which is exactly the kind of source-level inconsistency a useful BTCFi brief should flag for buyers instead of flattening into one headline number. [Babylon](https://babylonlabs.io/) · [Finality Providers](https://babylonlabs.io/finality-providers)
5. **Liquid still matters strategically** even when it does not fit cleanly into DeFi-style TVL comparisons. Its own positioning is closer to Bitcoin capital markets and settlement infrastructure than to a simple DeFi leaderboard entry. [Liquid Network](https://liquid.net/)
6. **The main research challenge is metric quality, not category importance.** A useful BTCFi brief must separate durable traction from campaign- or bridge-distorted numbers.
